Years before the construction of the High Line, another elevated line was transforming neighborhoods, although not to the same effect. In Morningside Heights, the Ninth Avenue El, the first elevated railway in New York City, was shaping the physicality and economy of a particular block. It’s tall structure, towering over 100 feet above 110th Street, the station would become known as the “Suicide Curve,” which affected many of the businesses surrounding the curve. Although long gone, traces of the existence of the El can still be seen in Block 1845 of Manhattan in the low elevation commercial buildings that exist there today.
